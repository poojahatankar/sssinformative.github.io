Planets Line Up

Main | Dwarf | Hypothetical



PLUTO
Pluto is a complex world of ice moutains and frozen plains. Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto is the best known of a new class of worlds called dwarf planets.


CERES
Ceres makes up almost the third of the asteroid belt's total mass, but it is still far smaller than Earth's moon. Ceres is heavily cratered with large amount of ice underground.


MAKEMAKE
It takes 310 Earth years for Makemake to complete one orbit around our Sun.


HAUMEA
Haumea is on the fastest rotating large objects in our solar system.


ERIS
Eris is one of the largest known drawf planets in our solar system. It's about the same size as pluto, but it is three times farther from the Sun. One orbit takes about 557 years.
